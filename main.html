North American Coastal Access Alliance

About Us

Empowering and supporting the creation of local family surf clubs to ensure that ocean safety, marine and coastal cultural education, social and emotional learning, surf community building, and surf education are accessible to all. Developing, maintaining, and providing a blueprint to run a regular coach supported bi-Weekly afterschool youth surf club, promoting a community surf build events, providing comprehensive and complimentary at home instructional content, on-location direct instruction, with in-water supervised surf experimentation.

Mission

Supporting the physical, emotional, social, and educational well-being of youth and families. Empowering communities to launch surf clubs rooted in equity, ocean education, social-emotional learning, and family engagement. Through inclusive surf and outdoor programs, combined with ocean safety, marine science education, social-emotional learning, opportunities to travel and experience new environments, community service projects, and family participation, we foster resilience, confidence, stewardship, and a community grounded in joy, curiosity, equity, and respect for the natural world.

Vision

We envision a future where every coastal community offers young people and families—regardless of background or resources—a deep sense of belonging in the ocean and outdoors. Through community-based surf clubs and educators, participants gain the knowledge to navigate and care for marine environments, develop tools for self-awareness and emotional resilience, and experience the joy of surfing. These experiences ripple outward, building confidence, resilience, and broadened perspectives that extend into all areas of life. Parents and families are essential partners in this journey, contributing time and care through discussion of materials and concepts, as well as shared ocean experiences that strengthen community, family bonds, and connection to the natural world.

Moonstone Family Surf Club

The Afterschool Surf Club is a parent-supported youth program that introduces kids ages 5–13 to the joy and challenge of surfing. Through in-water supervised sessions at Moonstone Beach, students build balance, ocean awareness, and self-confidence while learning to read the waves and stay safe in the water. Each 12-week session (starting mid-March and mid-August) runs bi-weekly from 3:30–6:00 PM, with additional 'Surf Community Days' to bring families together for shared time in the ocean. Our focus is on skill development, teamwork, and fostering a lifelong relationship with the sea.

The Surf School at Home program complements beach sessions with a 12-week at-home learning track. Families engage together in short, meaningful activities that explore:

  • Ocean safety and surf science
  • Environmental stewardship and coastal ecology
  • Cultural connections to the sea
  • Emotional awareness and self-regulation

This “flipped classroom” approach encourages parents and kids to learn, talk, and explore together before applying their knowledge at the beach. By blending home learning with ocean practice, we nurture capable, caring, and ocean-minded young surfers.
